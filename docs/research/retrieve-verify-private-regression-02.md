# Retrieve–verify private regression 02

A private Linear evaluator reran the documented protocol against commit `80555ac` after initially
testing an older build. The rerun changed the release conclusion from revise to qualified ship:
objective separation prevented typed relationships from monopolizing the weekly queue, isolated the
known graph under `structure`, and surfaced a genuine no-edge completed-work echo corroborated by
shared code surfaces. No private issue text, identifiers, paths, or credentials are retained here.

The evaluator also found two precision defects in the explicit discovery objectives:

- direct parent/child pairs could clear the semantic threshold and consume `overlap` slots even
  though the tracker already records that hierarchy;
- a broadly worded closed record could recur as the echo target for several active records.

This checkpoint excludes direct parent/child pairs from explicit overlap discovery while preserving
legacy default behavior. It also applies a configurable completed-target diversity cap of two,
retains qualified alternative echoes until selection, and backfills the queue deterministically.
Reports publish qualified, admitted, and cap-omitted counts for repeated targets without issue text.

The public fixture grew from six to ten judgments. With the pinned Potion model, rating-2 pairs kept
their 0.804 mean field-aware score. The six rating-0 cases averaged 0.514 after adding direct-hierarchy
and repeated-target cases; the highest new cases remained below the 0.72 echo and 0.82 overlap
thresholds. The field-wise MaxSim path still raised one command-vocabulary hard negative and remains
experimental.

Paired regressions on the three public evaluator corpora retained full queue sizes and lane mixes.
Compared with the preceding experimental output, candidate membership was 19/19 for Ralph TUI,
17/20 for opencode-beads, and 19/20 for Morphir. The target cap omitted one, four, and one repeated
echo pairs respectively while considering deterministic replacements. The bounded fallback depth
reduced the unbounded prototype's opencode pre-cap pool from 1,972 to 660 without changing selected
membership. Cold and warm candidate
arrays were byte-identical, and all three repositories retained clean Git-status fingerprints.

This is ready for the private evaluator to rerun the same objective protocol and rate the replacement
pairs. It does not promote field-aware retrieval to the default, establish full-corpus precision, or
satisfy the separate two-genuine-worktree collision gate.
